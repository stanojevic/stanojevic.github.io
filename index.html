Hello

My name is Miloš and I'm a PhD candidate (update: thesis submitted :) ) at the Institute for Logic, Language and Computation at University of Amsterdam working on Machine Translation under supervision of Prof. Khalil Sima'an .

At the moment I'm doing internship at Nuance lab in Sunnyvale, California with Prof. Edward Stabler.

My research interests lie mostly in the area of syntactic parsing and machine translation. In the parsing part of my research I like to use rich grammar formalisms such as Minimalist Grammars and CCG that can capture complex syntactic phenomena (displacement, control, agreement etc.). As for the machine translation part, I find two aspects of MT particularly interesting: reordering and evaluation.

In the MT evaluation part of my research I have constructed the BEER evaluation metric BEER. Yes, it is "yet another evaluation metric", but it works really well. It is trained for correlation with humans, has shown consistently high correlation accross last 4 years of WMT campains (WMT14, WMT15, WMT16, WMT17) both on sentence and corpus level and, unlike other trained metrics, it's easy to use and fast to run.

My PhD thesis topic is concerned mostly with MT reordering and evaluation. I try to induce the hierarchical structure present in word alignments in an unsupervised way; as, for example, in this paper. Using this hierarchical structure (which is similar to syntactic structure but not the same) we can improve machine translation in different aspects. The ones I concentrate on are reordering and evaluation.

In short, my view is that it is wrong to ignore the hierarchical structure present in language (this happens very often in MT and other areas of NLP) and it is fun to learn this structure in unsupervised way. I would tell you more, but it would require more words, time and beer.
